# Phase 4: The Resin Pour (Direct Bond Method)

> **Status: Planned — may change based on actual experience.** This phase has not been executed yet. The steps below are the planned approach and will be revised once the work is underway.

## 1. Scuff the Skin

Lightly sand the cured fiberglass skin (around the cells) with 120-grit paper and wipe with acetone. This ensures the new flood coat chemically bonds to the back skin.

## 2. The Seal Coat (Bubbles)

Mix ~16 oz of epoxy. Pour a thin skim coat over the fronts (blue side) of the solar cells. Use a brush to physically dab resin under the edges of the cells to push trapped air out. Wait for this to cure to a gel state (approx 3-4 hours).

## 3. The Flood Coat

Mix a large batch of epoxy (do not mix more than 32 oz at a time to prevent the bucket from overheating). Pour it into the frame over the front of the cells.

## 4. The Expansion Gap (CRITICAL)

Pour the resin until it is **1/16" below the top lip of the steel frame**. Do not let the resin touch the top edge.

## 5. Torch the Bubbles

Lightly pass a torch over the surface to pop any remaining air bubbles. Keep the torch moving and away from the bus wires.

## 6. Cure

Let the panel sit perfectly level for 48 hours. The clay mound will prevent any resin from leaking out the bottom holes.
