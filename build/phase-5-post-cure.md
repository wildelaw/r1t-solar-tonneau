# Phase 5: Post-Cure, Expansion Cap & J-Box Soldering

## 1. Demold

Lift the panel off the melamine board. Flip it over. You will see the clay mounds on the back, with the flat bus wires sticking out.

## 2. Remove the Clay

Pick the clay out of the holes. It will peel away cleanly, leaving perfectly sealed, flush holes around the bus wires.

## 3. The Expansion Cap

Flip the panel back over (sun-side up). Run a smooth bead of marine silicone into the 1/16" empty gap between the cured resin and the top edge of the steel frame. Tool it smooth with a gloved finger. This flexible cap allows the steel to expand in the heat without cracking the rigid resin.

## 4. Mount the J-Box

Apply a thick bead of silicone adhesive to the flange of your direct-solder J-Box. Press it onto the back of the fiberglass panel, directly over the two wire exit holes, 2 inches away from the steel frame.

## 5. Solder

Trim the flat bus wires to length inside the J-Box and solder them directly to the terminal pads inside the box. Seal the J-Box lid.
