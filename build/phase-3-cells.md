# Phase 3: Cell Placement & Routing

## 1. Insulate the Frame Edge

Solar cells cannot touch bare steel or they will short out and crack under flex. Run a strip of Kapton or packing tape along the inside bottom lip of the steel frame.

## 2. Lay the Cells (Face Up)

Place your solar cells **face-up** (blue side facing the ceiling) onto the cured fiberglass skin.

## 3. Solder Strings

Solder your cells together in your desired series/parallel configuration.

## 4. Route the Bus Bars

Solder your main positive and negative flat bus bars to the cell strings. Leave the ends of the bus bars about 4 inches too long. Push these excess ends down *through* the two drilled holes in the back skin so they stick out the bottom of the panel.

## 5. Seal the Holes (The Clay Method)

From the *inside* (top) of the panel, pack non-sulfur modeling clay tightly around the bus wires where they pass through the fiberglass. Build a solid 1-inch mound. *Do not use standard sulfur-based clay, as sulfur inhibits epoxy curing.*
